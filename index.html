Question 01 — Commitment

How committed have you been to this 4U English course thus far? Provide specific examples.

CommitmentFeedbackLiterary LensesInitiative

I think I've been pretty dedicated to Grade 12 English, and I believe that stems from how I approach assignments and feedback. An example of this would be how I work with literary lenses. I always attempt to make an effort to go beyond just a surface-level interpretation; I try to deepen my understanding of lenses by exploring their complexity and subfields for which the Glossary is super helpful. I believe that this makes my work stand out, it makes it interesting and flavorful, which is something I strive for. In addition to that, I take my feedback very seriously, I actively try to incorporate feedback into my work by treating it as an important factor in how I approach each assignment, for example the Timed Write provided a really good insight about how I should present and explain my points, which is something I applied towards my Formal Paragraph. I think feedback is super underrated.

For the days I am away I always take the initiative to catch up on my work. For example if I miss a chapter of Frankenstein or I don't necessarily understand what an assignment is asking I try to ask a peer for clarification. When it comes to a missed reading it gives me an excuse to throw on an audiobook and just chill. I often enjoy catching up on readings.

"Feedback is super underrated."

Question 03 — Growth

Which assignment could have used more work? What prevented optimal performance?

Oral ResponsePlanningSpeaking ClarityGrowth
◈
An honest answer follows

I think my weakest assignment was my oral response off of Paper Menagerie, I felt that I didn't give enough time or attention leading me to feel rushed. Looking back, I definitely should've made an outline of what I wanted to talk about. Prior planning would have allowed me to be better prepared and maybe even help with my speaking-clarity as I'm not constantly thinking about what I have to say next.

I definitely underestimated how different real responses feel than written responses. I feel that writing is very flexible and relaxing, a mistake can be fixed instantly and seeing your thoughts on paper gives you a space to brainstorm, but when you're speaking it's quite easy to 'run in circles' with your points.

I think my speaking clarity was fairly weak, I think this is the result of me not really being used to talking in front of the camera so it felt really awkward.

Moving forward, I plan to prepare thoroughly before any oral response or major assignment, whether that means creating a detailed outline, rehearsing my delivery, or simply giving myself more time to organize my thoughts before speaking.

"Recognizing the weak point is the first step toward strengthening it."

Question 05 — The Plan

How will you overcome these challenges to achieve greater success?

ReadingPattern RecognitionTime ManagementPlanning

The challenges I face in English class have always been pretty much the same. Oftentimes I feel that my writing ability isn't where I want it to be. I think Reading is the best solution to this problem, at least for me and here is why:

The Pattern Recognition Connection
∑

I've always loved mathematics and physics. One of the reasons I find these subjects so intriguing is about pattern recognition that is present across these two fields, and after doing a problem I train my brain to build an internal model capable of solving even harder problems.

✦

I believe this has an overlap in English class. Through reading, I am exposed to patterns in sentence structure, vocabulary, and argument development, which gradually shape the way I write.

This reminds me of the first text we read in this class, the article by Northrop Frye. Frye also draws the same overlap with grammar and algebra!

Another way that I can improve in English class, like mentioned earlier, is to prepare early and have a plan. However there is another variable that is quite relevant here and that is time management, breaking down assignments is essential as it makes everything so much more approachable and less overwhelming. It gives opportunity and headspace to refine and revise key ideas, something essential in order to be successful.
